Valerie Y. Odeh-Couvertier

About

I am a Ph.D. student in the Industrial and Systems Engineering Department at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My research interests fall in the area of causal inference, data analysis, and risk prediction approaches from observational data, with a focus on healthcare applications. As part of my research, I collaborate with UW Health and I am particularly interested in answering what if? questions using causal inference to aid in the decision making of admission processes and care transitions. I expect to graduate from my Ph.D. program in May 2024.

Prior to UW Madison, I obtained my bachelor's and master's degree in Industrial Engineering at the University of Puerto Rico-Mayagüez (UPRM). During my master’s degree, I worked with the NSF Engineering Research Center for Cell Manufacturing Technologies (CMaT) to assess the sensitivity of machine learning algorithms to challenges typically found in omic datasets, such as high dimensionality and highly correlated predictors, and I mitigated these challenges through the development of a data-driven modeling pipeline for relating CAR-T cell phenotype to multi-omics information. Aside from research, I worked as an industrial engineer at HP Inc. and Eli Lilly del Caribe.
